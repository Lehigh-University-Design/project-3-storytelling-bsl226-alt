1ST ALL-STAR MVP

At the 2002 NBA All-Star Game held in his hometown of Philadelphia, Bryant scored a game-high 31 points to lead the Western Conference to a victory, earning his first of four career All-Star MVP awards. He was notably booed by the hometown crowd due to comments he had made during the previous season's NBA Finals against the 76ers, an event that became an iconic moment in itself.

LAKERS VS. KINGS

The path to the Finals included a gruelling and historic seven-game Western Conference Finals series against the Sacramento Kings. This hard-fought series, marked by high drama and clutch performances, pushed the Lakers to their limit before they ultimately prevailed in Game 7.

"THREE-PEAT"

The most significant achievement of the season was the Lakers winning their third consecutive NBA title, the last "three-peat" in major American professional sports. The Lakers swept the New Jersey Nets in the NBA Finals (4-0). In that series, 23-year-old Bryant averaged 26.8 points, 5.8 rebounds, and 5.3 assists per game, solidifying his crucial role alongside Shaquille O'Neal.
